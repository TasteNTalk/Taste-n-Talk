This delicious pasta carbonara is a classic Italian dish that combines simple ingredients for maximum flavor. The key is using high-quality ingredients and proper technique.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti or bucatini
  • 150g guanciale or pancetta, cubed
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 100g pecorino romano, freshly grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the guanciale over medium heat until crispy but not burnt, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, in a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, whole eggs, and grated cheese. Season with plenty of black pepper.
  4. Cook the pasta until al dente. Before draining, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water.
  5. Add the drained pasta to the skillet with the guanciale and toss well to coat in the rendered fat.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly add the egg mixture, tossing continuously.
  7. Add a splash of the reserved pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce that coats the pasta.
  8. Serve immediately with additional grated cheese and black pepper.

Chef's Tips:

The key to perfect carbonara is working quickly once the pasta is cooked. The residual heat from the pasta will gently cook the eggs without scrambling them. Always remove the pan from heat before adding the egg mixture.

Traditional carbonara does not contain cream - the creaminess comes from the emulsion of eggs, cheese, and starchy pasta water. For authentic flavor, use guanciale if you can find it, otherwise pancetta is a good substitute.

About This Trend

Birria tacos have taken the food world by storm, moving from regional Mexican specialty to global sensation. These tacos feature tender, slow-cooked meat (traditionally goat but often beef) stewed in a rich, flavorful broth made with dried chilies and spices.

What makes birria tacos particularly special is that they're typically served "quesabirria" style - the tortillas are dipped in the fat from the top of the birria stew before being crisped on a griddle, often with cheese melted inside. They're served with a small bowl of the consommé for dipping.

Why It's Trending

The viral appeal of birria tacos can be attributed to several factors:

  • Visual appeal: The red-tinged tortillas and the act of dipping the taco into the consommé makes for extremely shareable social media content
  • Flavor complexity: The combination of tender meat, crispy tortilla, melted cheese, and rich broth offers a multi-dimensional eating experience
  • Food truck and pop-up friendly: Many successful businesses have launched focused almost exclusively on birria
  • Accessibility: While authentic birria requires time and specific ingredients, simplified versions have made it approachable for home cooks

Origin Story

Birria originated in Jalisco, Mexico, particularly in the town of Cocula. Traditional birria was made with goat meat, which was marinated in a paste of dried chilies, herbs, and spices, then slow-cooked in an underground pit. The dish was typically served at celebrations and special occasions.

The quesabirria taco variation likely developed in Tijuana and then gained enormous popularity when it migrated to Los Angeles food trucks and restaurants, before spreading nationwide and globally through social media.
